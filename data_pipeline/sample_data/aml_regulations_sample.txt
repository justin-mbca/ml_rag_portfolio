ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) REGULATIONS - SAMPLE DOCUMENT
===========================================================

1. INTRODUCTION

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations are a set of laws, regulations, and procedures
intended to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income.
Financial institutions are required to comply with AML regulations established by bodies
such as the Financial Action Task Force (FATF), FinCEN (USA), FCA (UK), and local regulators.

2. KEY AML COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
Financial institutions must:
- Identify and verify the identity of customers
- Identify beneficial owners
- Understand the nature and purpose of customer relationships
- Conduct ongoing monitoring of transactions

2.2 Know Your Customer (KYC)
All financial institutions must implement robust KYC procedures including:
- Identity verification using government-issued documents
- Address verification
- Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening
- Sanctions list screening (OFAC, UN, EU)

2.3 Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)
Institutions are required to:
- Monitor transactions for suspicious activity
- File Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) with FinCEN within 30 days
- Maintain SAR confidentiality
- Implement transaction monitoring systems

2.4 Record Keeping
Financial institutions must retain:
- Customer identification records for 5 years after account closure
- Transaction records for 5 years
- SAR filings and supporting documentation

3. RISK CATEGORIES

HIGH RISK:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- High-value transactions (>$10,000 cash)
- Wire transfers to high-risk jurisdictions
- Shell companies and complex ownership structures

MEDIUM RISK:
- Non-resident customers
- Business customers in high-risk industries
- Frequent large transactions

LOW RISK:
- Standard retail banking customers
- Regular payroll deposits
- Government entities

4. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

Violations of AML regulations can result in:
- Civil monetary penalties up to $1,000,000 per violation
- Criminal prosecution for responsible officers
- Regulatory enforcement actions
- Reputational damage

5. TECHNOLOGY REQUIREMENTS

Modern AML programs should leverage:
- AI/ML-based transaction monitoring
- Graph analytics for network analysis
- Real-time screening systems
- Automated SAR generation
